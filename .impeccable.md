## Design Context

### Users
Internal Nymbl product teams — both designers authoring the design system and engineers reviewing components before integrating them into NOS apps. Used on desktop browsers in a professional work context.

### Brand Personality
Precise, considered, grounded.

Physical object analogy: a Swiss engineering reference manual. Dense with meaning, nothing wasted. Confident typography, near-monochrome surface, brand color reserved for signal not decoration.

### Aesthetic Direction
Inspired by acctual.com. Light mode. The surface should feel like a precision instrument rather than a marketing page — calibrated, undecorated, immediately legible.

Anti-references: rounded-corner SaaS dashboards with gradient cards; color-heavy, icon-forward navigation; heavy use of the brand accent across non-interactive surfaces.

Font: Archivo (Google Fonts variable). Mechanical grotesque with a slightly condensed character — precise without being cold.

### Design Principles
1. **Neutrals dominate.** Brand indigo appears only on active states, primary actions, and the NOS mark. Semantic colors appear only on status indicators.
2. **Hierarchy through weight and size, not color.** Strong typographic contrast (40px page titles vs 10px tracked section labels) replaces decorative accents.
3. **Nothing rounded that doesn't need to be.** Sidebar active state is rectangular — no border-radius. Component and card radii from the token system apply everywhere else.
4. **Preview areas signal context.** Component demos use a subtle dot-grid background to communicate "this is a canvas" without decoration.
5. **Every label earns its pixel count.** Eyebrows, section labels, and category headings are at the smallest readable size — their presence is structural, not decorative.
